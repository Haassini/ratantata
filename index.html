Ratan Naval Tata

Early life and Education

  • Tata was born on December 28, 1937 in Mumbai, in one of the richest families.
  • He was raised by his grandmother, Lady Navajbai in the lap of luxury at Tata Palace
  • He went to Cornell University to study architecture and structural engineering.
  • Later he pursued a management course from Harvard University.

Business Career

  • In 1962, he joined the Tata Group and his first job involved working with the Tata Steel division in Jamshedpur.
  • He was appointed the Director-in-Charge of the National Radio & Electronics Company Limited (Nelco) in 1971
  • Tata later paved his way to become the Chairman of Tata Industries and was instrumental in ushering in a wide array of reforms.
  • It was under his leadership that Tata Consultancy Services went public and Tata Motors was listed in the New York Stock Exchange giving it more international power and recognition.

Awards and Honours

  • In year 2000, he was honored with "Padma Bhushan" by the government of India.
  • He was conferred an honorary doctorate in business administration by Ohio State University
  • He was also conferred honorary doctorate in technology by the Asian Institute of Technology, Bangkok

Philanthropy Activities

  • Tata set a perfect example of generosity and leadership during the 26/11 attacks.
  • He steered plan to provide cheap and clean drinking water and helped a few Pune-based designers develop a sub-Rs 1,000 water purifier, Swach.
  • Over two thirds of Tata Group is owned by charitable trusts that finance good causes.
